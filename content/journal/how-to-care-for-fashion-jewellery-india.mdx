**TL;DR** — Fashion jewellery in India fails for three predictable reasons: moisture (sweat, water, humidity), chemicals (perfume, deodorant, hairspray, cleaning agents), and friction (loose storage where pieces rub each other). Fix those three and almost any piece will look new for years. The single most important habit: **put your jewellery on last when getting ready, take it off first when getting home.**

## Why fashion jewellery loses its shine

Indian fashion jewellery — what shoppers also call imitation jewellery, costume jewellery, or artificial jewellery — is built around a base metal (usually brass or alloy) with a decorative finish on top. That finish is what gives a piece its colour, shine and stones. When the finish degrades, the piece looks "dull" or "black" — even though structurally it's fine.

Three forces wear down that finish faster than time alone:

1. **Moisture** — sweat (which is mildly acidic), tap water, shower steam, monsoon humidity.
2. **Chemicals** — perfumes and deodorants are the single biggest culprit; hairspray and skincare are next.
3. **Friction** — pieces stored loose in a drawer scratch each other every time you open it.

Heat and direct sunlight also matter for pieces with painted enamel (meenakari) or glued stones, but they're a distant fourth.

## The daily routine that doubles a piece's life

This takes ten extra seconds and is the single highest-impact habit:

> **Last on, first off.**
> Put jewellery on **after** you've applied perfume, deodorant, hairspray, lotion and makeup. Take it off **before** you cook, wash, exercise, sleep or shower.

That one rule eliminates almost every chemical and moisture contact your jewellery would otherwise see in a day.

After wear, wipe each piece down with a soft, dry cloth — a cotton handkerchief works, microfibre is better. This removes sweat and skin oils before they sit overnight.

## How to store fashion jewellery

The goal of storage is to keep pieces **dry, dark, and not touching each other.** A few options ranked from best to worst:

| Option | Why it works | Watch out for |
|---|---|---|
| Airtight zip pouch per piece | Limits air contact, prevents pieces touching | Don't pack damp pieces — trapped moisture is worse than open air |
| Lined jewellery box with compartments | Each piece gets its own slot | Cheap velvet linings can hold humidity in monsoon |
| Plastic organiser tray | Cheap, separates pieces | Air contact accelerates dulling slightly |
| Loose in a drawer | — | Friction will scratch finishes and bend prongs |

If you live in a coastal city (Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata, Kochi), drop a small **silica gel sachet** into the storage box. The ones that come with shoes and bags work fine — recharge them in the sun once a month.

For pieces you wear rarely, store the box inside a cupboard rather than on an open dressing table. Less light, less air movement, less damage.

## What to avoid (the things that quietly destroy fashion jewellery)

- **Water** — pools, showers, washing dishes, washing hands. The plating doesn't like being wet.
- **Perfume and deodorant sprayed directly onto neck/wrists** with jewellery already on.
- **Sleeping in your jewellery** — friction with sheets, sweat, and the bend-at-the-clasp problem.
- **Workouts** — sweat plus repeated movement is the fastest way to wear out earrings and pendants.
- **Cleaning the house** — bleach, surface cleaner, dishwashing liquid will strip plating in one contact.
- **Swimming pools** — chlorine is brutal on every finish.
- **Sea water** — salt is even worse.
- **Ultrasonic cleaners** — designed for solid gold; they'll loosen stones and damage glued elements on fashion pieces.

## How to clean fashion jewellery safely

Most fashion jewellery only needs a wipe with a soft dry cloth after each wear. For occasional deeper cleaning:

1. Mix a small bowl of **lukewarm water** with one drop of mild liquid soap.
2. Dampen a soft cloth (not the jewellery itself) in the solution.
3. Wipe along the metal — never scrub, never soak.
4. Pat dry immediately with a second dry cloth.
5. Let the piece air-dry on a paper towel for 15 minutes before storing.

**Never use** toothpaste, baking soda, lemon juice, vinegar or "jewellery cleaner" liquids on fashion jewellery. They're formulated for solid gold or silver and will strip the plating off a costume piece within seconds.

For stones (AD/American Diamond, crystal-style, pearl-style): clean only the metal around the stone. Avoid getting moisture under stones because most are glued, not set — water weakens the adhesive over time.

## Care by piece type

**Earrings** — biggest risk is moisture in the post hole. Wipe the posts after wear, never put them on when your earlobes are damp from showering.

**Necklaces & chains** — clasp them shut before storing so the chain doesn't tangle. Store flat or hung, not bunched in a corner.

**Rings** — the highest-friction piece you own. Take them off before any manual work — cooking, cleaning, gym, gardening. A ring you wear 24/7 will scratch and dull twice as fast as one you wear for outings only.

**Sets (necklace + earrings + ring)** — store the components together in one pouch so you don't lose pieces, but use a divider or paper insert so they don't rub.

## How long should a fashion jewellery piece last?

Honest answer: with careful use, an everyday fashion jewellery piece looks great for **12–24 months** of regular wear. Occasion pieces worn 3–5 times a year can look new for **3–5 years**. Pieces worn carelessly — straight into the shower, slept in, sprayed with perfume — may dull in **3–6 months**.

The math: a ₹599 set worn 20 times before it starts to dull is ₹30 per wear. With basic care, that drops to ₹5–₹10 per wear.

## Frequently asked questions

**Q: My fashion jewellery turned black overnight — is it ruined?**
Often, no. Black tarnish on the finish wipes off with a soft dry cloth. If wiping doesn't help, the underlying plating has worn through and the base brass is showing — that's structural and can't be reversed at home.

**Q: Can I wear fashion jewellery every day?**
Yes, but rotate. Wearing the same piece every single day puts continuous stress on the finish. Two or three pieces in rotation last several times longer combined than one piece worn daily.

**Q: Is fashion jewellery safe for sensitive skin?**
Most pieces are fine for most people, but if you have a known metal allergy, do a short patch test before wearing for long periods, and avoid contact with sweat and water.

**Q: How do I store fashion jewellery while travelling?**
A small zip pouch per piece, kept inside a hard glasses case or a plastic pill organiser. The hard outer case prevents bending and the inner pouches prevent friction.

**Q: Can I take fashion jewellery off and on while at a party?**
Try to avoid it. Repeated clasp-opening is one of the most common ways earring posts and necklace clasps fail.
